Encouragement

There are both wonderful promises and stern warnings as we study the verses of 2 Peter 1:1-11. There is also much good advice and encouragement.

1 Simon Peter, a servant and an apostle of Jesus Christ, to them that have obtained like precious faith with us through the righteousness of God and our Saviour Jesus Christ:

The apostle Peter begins his second epistle by humbly coming along side fellow believers to greet and assure us that we have been given, and share in, the same "precious faith" through the righteousness that we have in Jesus Christ. At no point does Peter laud his authority over us as an apostle but instead approaches, as a fellow servant, in love and humility. However, we mustn't allow his gentle approach to sway us from the gravity of his message.

PROMISE: Know that God has given us all we need for life and godliness.

3 According as his divine power hath given unto us all things that pertain unto life and godliness, through the knowledge of him that hath called us to glory and virtue:

Through His divine power God has given us all we need for life and godliness (2 Peter 1:3). God wants us to be partakers of the divine nature (godliness). His divine power is the source of the gifts that we are given.

Peter desperately wants us to understand this awesome promise: that not only does God want us to partake of His holy nature but He has also empowered us to attain it.

PROMISE: Know that God has called us to glory and excellence.

He has called us to live for His glory in moral excellence and then to be with Him in glory (2 Peter 1:3). It is "through knowledge", that is to say through knowing Him personally, and through knowing the certainty and empowering of His calling, that the gifts flow to us and are evidenced in us.

PROMISE: Know that we are given exceeding great and precious promises.

4 Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises: that by these ye might be partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption that is in the world through lust.

The gifts that are given to us are "exceeding great and precious promises" (2 Peter 1:4).

These promises are the rock of Christ on which we can stand and hold firm in any storm. They equip us to overcome the sinful desires we face every day. They're weapons for the battle for faith, love and purity.

PROMISE: Know that we are empowered to escape the sinful desires of the world.

It is through meditating on and believing and trusting in God's great and precious promises that we escape the corruption of the world by combatting and overcoming sinful desires. (2 Peter 1:4)

The sinful desires of the world promise us pleasure that they do not deliver in the long run but God's holy nature is to be so satisfied in glory and excellence that there is no temptation to sin whatsoever.

TODO: Become a partaker of the divine nature.

This is God's goal for us.

We become partakers of the divine nature when we escape the sinful desires and corruption of the world through the knowledge of God's glory and excellence. When we have tasted of this as a result of the great and precious promises then we defeat sinful desires. (2 Peter 1:4)

When we conquer the promises of the world, which are deceitful, we share in the divine godliness.

As true Christians it is our destiny to achieve this!

Liberating Promises Demand Action

For the very reason of these great God-given promises (2 Peter 1:3-4) the believer cannot be indifferent or self-satisfied. Such an abundance of divine grace calls for total dedication.

Even though God has poured His divine power into the believer, the Christian life is not lived to the honour of God without effort. We must not become lazy or complacent.

Believers are called to bear fruit knowing that trees which do not bring forth good fruit are "hewn down and cast into the fire" (Matthew 7:19).

It is for this reason that Peter commands the believer to diligently add to their faith and lists seven qualities (2 Peter 1:5-7) that are important for the believer to pursue in order to reach spiritual maturity and assure them self of their salvation.

The apostle makes it his duty insisting that it is right for us to be continually reminded about these things (2 Peter 1:12) and for us to keep them permanently in remembrance (2 Peter 1:15).

Seven qualities to pursue most diligently.

5 And beside this, giving all diligence, add to your faith virtue; and to virtue knowledge;
6 And to knowledge temperance; and to temperance patience; and to patience godliness;
7 And to godliness brotherly kindness; and to brotherly kindness charity.

The seven qualities described by Peter (2 Peter 1:5-7) to be added to our faith and pursued most diligently include:

  • Virtue (moral excellence and fortitude)
  • Knowledge
  • Temperance (self-control)
  • Patience
  • Godliness
  • Brotherly kindness
  • Charity (agape love)

The qualities listed are not meant to be approached in order. Each is to be pursued separately in its own merit. Whilst standing firm in our faith we must apply ourselves to grow spiritually in each quality, striving to advance and press in ever deeper. We need these additions to reach the fullness of Christ (see Ephesians 3:19, 4:13). These additions are the elements of the very nature of God Himself.

TODO: Add to your faith virtue.

Our faith is the starting point that we are to add to.

"Virtue" is not an easy word to understand. Searches yield the meaning as "moral excellence and fortitude, active quality or power, manly strength or courage; valour."

We must see "virtue" as not just power. No. It is the strength and power of God, emanating right out of His very heart through His Spirit into ours. It is He; it is a part of His divine nature; it is all about His strength and power stemming from His excellent goodness. This then is His power, which gives us now the strength and ability to go on the offensive against the devil and his tricks that block our road to immortality.

This first addition to the faith, then, is an offensive weapon given to us by God to go with our trust-faith-assurance in Him. "Virtue" then is that quality of valour, that makes us like the mighty men of war as David was. They were men of strength. Mighty men. Strong men and women in the Spirit, pro-active in their attacks on the enemy. This all comes in realising that it is God Almighty who does all this conquering – not only for us, but also in and through us.

TODO: Add to virtue knowledge.

"Knowledge" means understanding, correct insight, truth properly comprehended and applied. This quality involves a diligent study and pursuit of truth in both the Word of God and relationship with God.

Adding knowledge then is all about first learning His true plan and promises and incorporating them into our thinking. This takes true teachings, much study and much communication with our Saviour.

Knowledge is extremely important and necessary for godliness because it is through the knowledge of God and knowing the certainty of His calling to share in His glory and excellence that we receive the great and precious promises.

TODO: Add to brotherly kindness charity.

"Charity" here means "love" but this is no ordinary love, this is God's love, translated from the Greek word "agape" meaning an unconditional love demanding nothing in return. This is the same "charity" (love) that is talked about in 1 Corinthians 13.

It is this kind of love that we are to finally add to the divine nature within us now. "Agape love is the bond of perfectness." It is that last attribute of God's divine nature that makes us complete in Him. It matures us, for when added, we will have been "conformed to the image of His Son."

PROMISE: If you abound in doing these things you will not be unfruitful.

8 For if these things be in you, and abound, they make you that ye shall neither be barren nor unfruitful in the knowledge of our Lord Jesus Christ.

A tree is known by its fruit and Peter says if you abound in doing the above things then you will not be unfruitful nor will you be ineffective in your relationship with the Lord Jesus Christ (2 Peter 1:8).

In other words, the believer who pursues the spiritual qualities mentioned above proves to them self by spiritual fruit that they are called (Rom. 8:30; 1 Peter 2:21) and chosen (1 Peter 1:2) by God to salvation.

WARNING: Beware of spiritual amnesia.

9 But he that lacketh these things is blind, and cannot see afar off, and hath forgotten that he was purged from his old sins.

Peter warns about the believer who lacks the above qualities i.e. a believer who chooses not to diligently pursue them. He describes such a person as "blind, and cannot see afar off, and hath forgotten that he was purged from his old sins" (2 Peter 1:9).

To be "blind" is to shut your eyes to the truth that has been revealed in the Lord Jesus Christ. Such a person also "can't see afar off" i.e. they are short-sighted and they don't look to the future knowing full well where all things are heading: the judgement of God. Neither do they find the promises of God desirable. Instead they forget what Jesus has done for them. They forget that Jesus purged their old sins and go back to living the same way as before. They do not seek relationship with God and therefore lack knowledge becoming powerless. To live as the world lives after you are saved will draw you away from salvation.

A professing Christian who is making no effort to attain the virtues mentioned above is unable to discern their true spiritual condition and therefore can have no assurance of salvation. Such a person will have no confidence about their profession of faith. They may possess all the blessings of 2 Peter 1:3-4, but without the excellences of 2 Peter 1:5-7 they will live in doubt and fear.

Christians must never stand still.

The failure to diligently pursue these spiritual virtues produces spiritual amnesia. It is a very dangerous thing not to grow in God. The one-time experience of salvation is not enough. We are either growing in Christ or we are going backwards. We can never stand still.

Salvation is a daily walk through life, growing each day more like Jesus. To do anything else will not work. We can be blinded by the world, if we are not careful, and forget the forgiveness we received in Christ.

TODO: Examine yourself to see if you are in the faith.

Paul challenges the believer to "examine yourself" (see 2 Corinthians 13:5) and "prove to yourself" that you are in the faith.

As believers we must be confident that God's Holy Spirit dwells in us and bears witness to our spirit that we are children of God (see Romans 8:16). Don't let the enemy cause you to doubt based on your feelings. Ignore how you feel.

Instead rely on the truth of what God's Word says: recognise the good fruit you are bearing, your love for the Word of God, your hatred of sin, your love for your Christian brethren and your endless pursuit of knowledge and godliness. If you don't see these virtues then it's time to take Peter's following warning and advice to heart.

WARNING: Give diligence to make your calling and election sure.

10 Wherefore the rather, brethren, give diligence to make your calling and election sure: for if ye do these things, ye shall never fall:

Peter warns us that as believers we are not just to make our calling and election sure but we are to "give diligence" to confirm it (2 Peter 1:10). That means giving it our full attention without delay.

We are instructed by Paul to "work out your own salvation in fear and trembling" (Philippians 2:12-13) and to "run so as to obtain the prize" (1 Corinthians 9:24).

The Lord Jesus Himself too gives us the priority to "Seek ye first the kingdom of God and His righteousness" (Matthew 6:25-33) above worrying about all the other things we might need each day.

How can I make my calling and election sure?

Amongst the other favourite weapons of the enemy are "lies" and "doubt". The enemy wants to convince us that we are not saved and that we must do many things to earn God's election. Nothing could be further from the truth! God wants us to be assured of our salvation.

As believers we must trust the Word of God to overcome any doubts that we may have about our calling and acknowledge that God has already done everything necessary on our behalf to enable us to walk out that calling and thereby evidence our election.

Election is the free gift of God by grace and cannot be earned. It is God that works in us both to will and to do of His good pleasure (see Philippians 2:13).

TODO: Confirm your election and be assured of your salvation.

Though God is certain who His elect are, and has given them an eternally secure salvation that cannot be taken away, at times the individual Christian them self might not have such assurance of their salvation. For this reason, Peter explains how a believer can be assured and compells us to "give all diligence" to assure ourselves.

God predestined that all of the elect become like Jesus (see Romans 8:29). Therefore the reassuring confirmation that we are among the elect is that we are becoming like Jesus. We desire and strive continually to become Christ-like. As a result we witness the good fruit we bear and are known by it. This assures us of our salvation. We learn by doing!

Thus, the confirmation of our election is our progress in sanctification.

PROMISE: If ye do these things, ye shall never fall.

The apostle promises that "if ye do these things, ye shall never fall" (2 Peter 1:10).

As the Christian pursues the qualities listed by Peter (verses 5-7) and sees that their life is useful and fruitful (verse 8), they will not stumble into doubt, despair, fear or questioning but enjoy the full assurance of their salvation.

We don't do these things to earn our election but rather to confirm that we have been called.

11 For so an entrance shall be ministered unto you abundantly into the everlasting kingdom of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ.

PROMISE: Entrance will be given to you into the everlasting kingdom.

“Entrance … ministered unto you abundantly into the everlasting kingdom”: Dedicated Christians, who grow and become fruitful, cultivating through God’s provision and power the prime qualities of faith – virtue, knowledge, self-control, patience, godliness, brotherly kindness, and brotherly love – will never stumble or fall. Peter piles up the words to bring joy to the weary Christian's heart. An abundant entrance into eternal heaven is the hope and reality for a Christian who lives a faithful, fruitful life here on earth. Peter's point is that a Christian who pursues the list virtues (verses 5-7) will not only enjoy assurance in the present, but a full, rich reward in the future life (1 Cor. 4:5; Rev. 22:12). A full reward awaits the faithful, godly believer in Christ's ever-lasting kingdom (see Eph. 2:6-7).

Challenge

  • Have you grasped the exceeding great and precious promises given to you by God?
  • Are you making every effort to grow in moral excellence?
  • Are you making every effort to increase your knowledge of God's character and His will by living in relationship with Him?
  • Are you making every effort to strengthen your power of self-control over the passions in your life that would otherwise lead you astray?
  • Are you making every effort to enlarge your capacity for patience?
  • Are you making every effort to cultivate godliness to develop a heart for God?
  • Are you making every effort to grow warm in your affection for your fellow believers?
  • And are you making every effort to stir up love in your will for the person you dislike the most?
